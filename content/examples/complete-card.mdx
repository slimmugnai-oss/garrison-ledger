# TSP Contribution Basics

**BLUF:** The Thrift Savings Plan (TSP) is your federal 401(k) with rock-bottom fees. Max out the BRS match (5%), then increase contributions toward the annual limit.

## BRS Matching (5%)

Under the Blended Retirement System (BRS):
- DoD automatically contributes **1%** of base pay
- DoD matches your contributions up to **4%** more
- **Total possible match: 5%** of base pay

### Example (E-5 with <DataRef source="BAS" paygrade="E05" />)

If base pay is $3,000/month:
- Automatic 1% = $30
- If you contribute 5% = $150
- DoD matches 4% = $120
- **Total monthly TSP: $300** ($150 yours + $150 DoD)

## Action Steps

1. **Log in to [TSP.gov](https://www.tsp.gov)** and verify current contribution %
2. **Minimum:** Contribute 5% to get full match
3. **Optimal:** Increase % each pay raise until hitting annual limit
4. **Roth vs Traditional:** Consider tax bracket (consult tax professional)

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I change my contribution % anytime?**  
A: Yes, via myPay. Changes take 1-2 pay periods to process.

**Q: What happens to TSP when I PCS?**  
A: TSP stays with you - it's federal, not installation-based.

**Q: Do special pays (BAH/BAS) count toward TSP limit?**  
A: No - only base pay and special pays *included* in your taxable wages count toward the annual limit.
